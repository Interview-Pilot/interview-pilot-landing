# What to Say When Asked Why Are You Interested in This Role

If an interviewer asks **why are you interested in this role**, give a short answer that connects three things: your relevant skills, your career direction, and a specific reason the company or team makes sense for you. The strongest answers sound focused, not rehearsed.

A good response usually takes 30 to 60 seconds. You are not trying to prove you want the job badly enough. You are showing that you understand the role and can explain why it is a smart fit for both sides.

## Quick answer formula

Use this simple structure:

1. **Start with the role itself.** Say what part of the job matches your background or interests.
2. **Connect it to your skills.** Mention 1 to 2 strengths that help you succeed in this work.
3. **Tie it to your goals.** Explain why this role fits where you want to grow next.
4. **Add one specific company reason.** Refer to the team, product, mission, customers, or work style.

A basic template looks like this:

> I’m interested in this role because it matches my experience in [skill or function], and it gives me a chance to build on that work in [area you want to grow]. I’m also drawn to [company detail], because it lines up with the kind of environment where I do my best work.

That is the core of a strong answer to **why are you interested in this position**.

## Why interviewers ask this question

This question is not just small talk. Hiring managers use it to check a few things at once:

- Whether you understand the job
- Whether your motivation is genuine
- Whether your background fits the opening
- Whether you are likely to stay engaged after hiring
- Whether you can communicate clearly under pressure

They are listening for alignment, not a dramatic pitch. If your answer is too generic, the interviewer may assume you applied without much thought. If your answer is too focused on what you want from the job, they may worry you are ignoring the employer’s needs.

A strong answer shows balance: you want the role because you can contribute to it and grow in it.

## The best answer formula: skill, goal, fit

The easiest way to answer **why are you interested in this role** is to build your response around three parts.

| Part | What to include | What it sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| Skill | Relevant experience or strengths | "I’ve done similar work in project coordination and client communication." |
| Goal | What you want to do next | "I want to take on more ownership and work closer to strategy." |
| Fit | Why this company or team appeals to you | "Your focus on customer experience is especially appealing to me." |

You do not need to cover every possible detail. In most interviews, one sentence for each part is enough.

## Interview answer examples you can adapt


![Illustration for Interview answer examples you can adapt in What to Say When Asked Why Are You Interested in This Role](/static/blog-generated/why-are-you-interested-in-this-role-inline-1.webp)
Below are practical **interview answer examples** for different situations. Use them as a starting point, then customize them with your own experience.

### Example 1: General professional role

> I’m interested in this role because it lets me use the analytical and communication skills I’ve built in my current job, while also giving me more responsibility on cross-functional projects. I like that your team works closely with both operations and customer success, because that’s the kind of environment where I’ve done my best work.

Why it works:

- It names specific strengths
- It shows a next-step career goal
- It gives a real reason the company stands out

### Example 2: Early-career or entry-level role

> I’m interested in this position because it gives me a chance to build a strong foundation in the work I want to grow into long term. I’ve already developed strong attention to detail and communication skills through school and internships, and I’m excited to apply them in a role where I can keep learning from experienced teammates.

Why it works:

- It sounds motivated without sounding vague
- It does not overstate experience
- It shows willingness to learn and contribute

### Example 3: Career change answer

> I’m interested in this role because it brings together the parts of my background that I most enjoy, especially problem-solving, client interaction, and process improvement. While my path has included different responsibilities, I’ve consistently been drawn to work where I can help teams run more effectively, and this role is a strong match for that direction.

Why it works:

- It explains the transition without apologizing for it
- It connects past work to the new role
- It keeps the focus on transferable value

### Example 4: Leadership or management role

> I’m interested in this role because it combines team leadership with the opportunity to improve how work gets done. I’ve enjoyed coaching others, building structure, and removing blockers for the team, and this position seems like a good fit for that strength as well as my goal of taking on broader ownership.

Why it works:

- It shows leadership motivation
- It links leadership to practical outcomes
- It signals readiness for responsibility

## How to tailor your answer for phone, video, and in-person interviews

The question is the same, but the delivery can change depending on the format.

### Phone interview

On a phone screen, keep it crisp and structured. The recruiter may be screening for fit, not asking for a long story.

Best approach:

- Use 2 to 4 sentences
- Lead with the most relevant match
- Mention one company-specific reason

Example:

> I’m interested in this role because it matches my background in customer support and process improvement. I’ve been looking for a position where I can take on more ownership, and your focus on service quality stood out to me.

### Video interview

On video, you can be slightly more conversational, but still stay concise. Make eye contact, speak clearly, and avoid reading a script word for word.

Best approach:

- Sound natural
- Keep your energy steady
- Pause between the skill, goal, and fit parts

### In-person interview

In person, you can add a little more warmth or context if the interviewer is engaged. Still, do not drift into a long explanation.

Best approach:

- Start with the direct answer
- Add one concrete detail from your background
- End with a company-specific reason

## What not to say

Many candidates lose this question by being too broad, too self-focused, or too scripted. Avoid these common mistakes.

| Mistake | Weak example | Better approach |
|---|---|---|
| Too generic | "I just need a job and this one looked good." | Explain why the role fits your skills and goals |
| Too self-centered | "I need better pay and a shorter commute." | Focus on contribution and growth |
| Too vague | "I like helping people and working hard." | Name the actual work you enjoy doing |
| Too scripted | "I’ve always been passionate about excellence and synergy." | Use plain language and real details |
| Too much flattery | "Your company is the best in the world." | Mention one specific reason the team or role appeals to you |

A useful rule: if your answer could apply to almost any job, it is not specific enough.

## How to make your answer sound genuine

Your answer should not sound like a memorized speech. It should sound like a thoughtful, simple explanation of your interest.

To make it more authentic:

- Use words you normally use
- Reference actual experience from your resume
- Mention a real reason you applied
- Keep the answer short enough to say naturally
- Avoid exaggeration or overexplaining

A genuine answer often includes one concrete detail, such as:

- A type of work you enjoy
- A skill you want to use more often
- A team structure you work well in
- A product, mission, or customer group you care about

## A simple framework you can remember

If you freeze during the interview, remember this short pattern:

**I can do the work. I want the next challenge. This company is a fit.**

You can turn that into a natural response like this:

> I’m interested in this role because I know I can contribute using my experience in reporting and stakeholder communication. I’m also ready for a role with more ownership, and this team’s focus on collaboration makes it especially appealing.

That one structure works for many industries and experience levels.

## Sample answers by situation

Here are a few more **how to answer interview questions** examples you can adapt quickly.

### If you are applying to a similar role

> I’m interested in this role because it builds directly on the work I’m already doing, but with more opportunity to make an impact. I’ve had success with the core responsibilities here, and I’m excited by the chance to apply those skills in a setting where I can grow further.

### If the job is a step up

> I’m interested in this role because it feels like the right next step in my career. I’ve developed a solid base in the current level of work, and I’m ready to take on more responsibility, especially in areas like planning, coordination, and team collaboration.

### If the company mission matters to you

> I’m interested in this role because the work connects to a mission I care about, and I want my day-to-day work to contribute to something meaningful. I also like that this role would let me use my background in a way that supports both the team and the people you serve.

### If you are changing industries

> I’m interested in this role because it gives me a way to apply transferable skills in a field I’m genuinely excited about. I’ve done well in roles that require organization, communication, and problem-solving, and I’m eager to build on those strengths in this new industry.

## How to prepare your own answer in 5 minutes

Use this quick prep process before your interview:

1. Read the job description and highlight the top 3 responsibilities.
2. Pick 1 or 2 of your strongest matching skills.
3. Decide what kind of growth this role gives you.
4. Find one specific company detail you genuinely like.
5. Put those pieces into a 30-second answer.

You can write it as:

- **I’m a fit because...**
- **I want this role because...**
- **This company stands out because...**

That is often enough to create a strong response without sounding rehearsed.

## Final answer template

If you want one flexible script, use this:

> I’m interested in this role because it matches my background in [skill or experience], and it gives me a chance to do more of [type of work you enjoy]. I’m also looking for a role where I can keep growing in [career goal], and I’m especially drawn to [company or team detail] because it fits the kind of environment where I do my best work.

Make it shorter if needed. Add only the details that matter. The goal is clarity, not completeness.

## Key takeaways

- Keep your answer short, direct, and specific.
- Connect your skills, your goals, and the company fit.
- Use plain language, not buzzwords.
- Customize your answer for the role and interview format.
- Avoid generic or overly self-focused responses.
